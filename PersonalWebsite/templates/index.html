Welcome to my website!

My name is Marnie, and I'm a STEM educator. I've taught biology, chemistry, physics, and environmental science at the middle school level. As a Master's candidate at the Harvard Graduate School of Education, I have been learning and applying knowledge towards teaching computer science.

I believe that students achieve their best when provided with both high expectations and high support. To this end, I have utilized platforms like Canvas to create dynamic, interactive web pages for my classes full of practice quizzes, simulations, and other learning resources such as Quizlets, GoFormative, and more. By offering abundant resources for success in a non-judgemental, encouraging environment, I ensure every student feels empowered to reach their learning goals.

Before teaching, I was a communications assistant at The Hastings Center, a bioethics research institute, where I explored topics ranging from reproductive ethics to sports ethics. For the past two summers, I have worked in partnership with the Materials Science Department at Northwestern University on translating STEM activities to blind and low vision learners.

Outside of teaching, I love biking, hiking, doing yoga, and walking my dog. I'm a huge Survivor nerd and I love learning languages, including Spanish, Hebrew, Arabic, and American Sign Language.
